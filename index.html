Maps Agents Game Modes

VALORANT Agents as of November 2025

Duelists

Jett

Exceptional agility and vertical mobility. Jett excels at quick entries, flanking, and out-aiming opponents. Her mobility allows her to take high-risk positions and escape if things go south. Great for aggressive players who rely on aim and movement.

Phoenix

Uses fire-based abilities to flash, blind, and heal himself. Good for planting/defusing with utility, self-sustain under pressure, and aggressive pushes. Effective for players who want versatility and survivability alongside fragging potential.

Reyna

Gains strong buffs after kills (healing, invisibility, etc.), rewarding aggressive play and clutch potential. Her strength scales heavily with aim and kills — excels in 1v1s and aggressive peeks. Risky if unable to secure kills, but high reward for confident fraggers.

Raze

Uses grenades, boombot, and explosive weapons to deal heavy area damage — perfect for clearing corners, forcing enemies out of cover, and pushing through choke points. Great against grouped enemies or in tight spaces, and can disrupt enemy setups.

Yoru

Utilizes fake footsteps, teleport/flash devices to trick enemies and create openings. Ideal for mind games, site entries, flanks, and surprising enemies. Good for strategic, cunning players rather than brute fragging or raw aim.

Neon

Fast movement and burst abilities make Neon excellent for rapid pushes, aggressive site takes, and quick peeks. Her high-speed plays are risky but rewarding for confident, high-tempo players who like to surprise enemies and snowball rounds.

Iso

Mixes shield-based defense and aggressive toolkit. Offers a balance of survivability and fragging potential; good for players who want a hybrid style between brute aggression and tactical positioning.

Waylay

Designed for flexible aggressive plays. Great for unpredictable push strategies and dynamic duels. Useful for players who like to adapt on the fly and catch enemies off-guard.

Initiators

Sova

Uses recon tools (arrows, darts) to reveal enemy positions and gather information; essential in coordinated plays and site entries. Great for teams needing intel before committing to a push or defending a retake.

Breach

Excels at clearing sites with flash, stun, and concuss abilities. Great for breaking enemy setups, forcing them out of corners, and enabling teammates to enter. Good for coordinated or team-based playstyles.

Skye

Provides flashes, healing, and recon — supports aggression and team pushes. Excellent for players who like to help teammates while still enabling aggressive plays, especially in flexible or adaptive team comps.

KAY/O

Suppresses enemy abilities, negating their utility while still providing entry tools. Useful against heavy ability-reliant enemy teams or when you want to neutralize utility threats and gain tempo.

Fade

Uses vision-blocking, debuffs, and disruption tools to disorient or flush out enemies. Great for aggressive site entries, retakes, or denying defensive setups — especially effective if coordinated with teammates.

Gekko

Deploys pet-like utilities that can concuss, blind, or damage enemies. Offers unique disruption and entry potential – ideal for chaotic pushes or surprise plays. Useful for players who like creativity and unpredictability.

Tejo

A modern initiator with gadget-based utility (e.g. drones), offering recon, disruption, and flexible entry tactics. Effective for coordinated or strategic plays that require planning and teamwork.

Controllers

Brimstone

Provides smokes and area-denial tools, useful for blocking sightlines, protecting pushes, or defending sites. Good for strategic control and supporting teammates during executes or defenses.

Omen

Offers versatile smokes and teleport/flank tools, allowing for creative plays, surprise attacks, and site control. Effective for players who enjoy strategic positioning, deception and flexible playstyles.

Viper

Uses poison clouds and area-denial tools to block or control enemy movement, especially effective in post-plant or hold situations. Great for delaying pushes and controlling map flow.

Astra

Offers global map-control abilities that can shape how sites are attacked or defended. Excellent for strategic plays, coordination, and controlling enemy rotations or utility usage.

Harbor

Utilizes water-based tools for slowing, zoning, and controlling enemy movement — useful for site control, delaying pushes, or defending bomb sites. Great for tactical, controlled team play.

Clove

Combines smokes/utility and flexible utility for both attack and defense. Ideal for players who like a hybrid control style and value adaptability. Can fit many team compositions well.

Sentinels

Sage

Provides healing, slows, and defensive support — ideal for anchoring, supporting team during holds, and clutching through revives/defensive utility. Great for supportive players who want to help teammates and control defensive rounds.

Cypher

Uses traps, cameras, and alarms to gather intel and defend sites. Great for passive hold strategies, catching flanks, and controlling rotations — ideal for cautious and strategic players.

Killjoy

Deploys gadgets like turrets, traps and alarms to lock down sites, delay enemy pushes, and secure post-plant situations. Excellent for defensive players and site anchoring roles.

Chamber

Combines long-range weaponry with traps and teleport tools — useful for controlling sightlines, anchoring sites, and punishing aggressive enemies. Good for players who prefer precision, passive defense, and tactical positioning.

Deadlock

Uses suppression-based utility and defensive tools — ideal for controlling enemy pushes and supporting team during clutch defensive rounds. Flexible, especially in coordinated or strategic team compositions.

Vyse

Offers a mix of defensive and supportive tools, suited for flexible team compositions and adaptive playstyles. Good for players who like a hybrid sentinel role and want to adapt to different round types.
